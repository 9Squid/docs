These guides walk you through the complete set of Buildmarkets integration workflows — from opening your first investor account to placing trades and reading portfolio performance. Each guide covers a discrete step in the investing lifecycle, with working curl examples, request and response schemas, and decision points you'll need to handle in your product.

Buildmarkets supports two primary integration paths. **Self-directed investing** gives your end users direct control over their brokerage accounts — they fund their accounts, pick their own securities, and place orders themselves. **Wealth management** adds advisory infrastructure on top: risk assessments, financial goals, investment policies, and advisor-managed portfolios. Both paths share the same investor onboarding, KYC, funding, and trading primitives; they differ in what you build on top.

## Environments

| Environment | Base URL |
|-------------|----------|
| Sandbox | `https://dev-tapp-api.tappengine.com` |
| Production | `https://api.tappengine.com` |

Every request requires `X-API-Key` and `X-API-Secret` headers. Use your sandbox credentials during development and switch to production credentials when you go live.

## Integration patterns

### Webhooks over polling

Configure a webhook endpoint via `POST /v1/webhooks` and subscribe to the event types relevant to your product. Webhooks are the right choice for operational state changes — KYC approval, order fills, deposit settlement. Polling is acceptable for UI-driven reads like current balances or position values.

### Pending state transitions

Every major resource in Buildmarkets starts in a pending state before becoming active. Plan your UI around these transitions:

| Resource | Pending state | Active state |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Investor account | `onboarding` | `active` |
| Funding source | `pending` | `active` |
| Deposit | `pending` | `completed` |
| Order | `pending` | `filled` |

### `corr_metadata` for cross-system linking

The `corr_metadata` field on an investor record accepts a freeform string up to 255 characters. Store your internal user ID or account reference there at creation time. This eliminates the need for a separate ID-mapping database and makes cross-system lookups straightforward.
