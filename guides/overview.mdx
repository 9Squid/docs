## 1. Embedded brokerage app

**Scenario:** You're building a consumer fintech app and want to add an "Invest" tab that lets users open a brokerage account, fund it, and trade stocks and ETFs.

**What you'll build:**

1. Collect KYC data and open a brokerage account ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts`
2. Link the user's external bank account via ACH ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/ach-relationships`
3. Initiate an initial cash deposit ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/funding/deposits`
4. Display a stock or ETF search using reference data ŌĆö `POST /v1/marketdata/reference/symbol-details`
5. Place a market or limit order ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders`
6. Display the portfolio ŌĆö balances and open positions ŌĆö `GET /v1/accounts/{accountId}/balances` + `GET /v1/accounts/{accountId}/positions`

**Key account fields:**
- `account_type: "individual"`
- `investment_profile`
- `agreements`

**Key order fields:**
- `symbol`
- `asset_class` (`equity` or `etf`)
- `order_type`
- `side`
- `quantity` or `notional`
- `client_order_id`

## 2. Advisor-managed accounts

**Scenario:** You operate a wealth management platform where registered advisors manage portfolios on behalf of clients.

**What you'll build:**

1. Open a client account with advisor metadata ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts`
2. Fund the client account via ACH ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/funding/deposits`
3. Place and manage orders on the client's behalf ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders`
4. Modify or cancel working orders ŌĆö `PATCH /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders/{orderId}` / `DELETE /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders/{orderId}`
5. Monitor portfolio value across all client accounts ŌĆö `GET /v1/accounts/{accountId}/balances` + `GET /v1/accounts/{accountId}/positions`
6. Receive real-time fill confirmations via webhook ŌĆö `POST /v1/webhooks`

**Key account fields:**
- `discretionary_account: true` ŌĆö grants the advisor full trading authority
- `registered_rep_code` ŌĆö associates with a registered representative
- `advisor_code` ŌĆö associates with a specific advisor

**Key order fields:**
- Use `client_order_id` on every order to prevent duplicate submissions
- Orders support `market`, `limit`, `stop`, and `stop_limit` types for equities and ETFs

## 3. Robo-advisor / automated investing

**Scenario:** You're building an automated investing product where users define goals and risk tolerance and your system manages their portfolio using rules-based logic.

**What you'll build:**

1. Open an account capturing detailed suitability data ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts`
2. Enable automatic dividend reinvestment ŌĆö `dividend_reinvestment: true` on account creation
3. Fund accounts on a recurring schedule via ACH ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/funding/deposits`
4. Submit dollar-based orders to implement your allocation model ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders` with `notional`
5. Rebalance by cancelling and replacing working orders ŌĆö `DELETE /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders/{orderId}` + `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders`
6. React to order fill events to trigger the next rebalancing step ŌĆö `POST /v1/webhooks` (subscribe to order events)

**Key account fields:**
- `investment_profile.risk_tolerance` ŌĆö `conservative`, `moderate`, `aggressive`
- `investment_profile.investment_objective` ŌĆö e.g. `growth`, `income`, `capital_preservation`
- `investment_profile.time_horizon` ŌĆö `short`, `average`, `long`
- `dividend_reinvestment` ŌĆö auto-reinvests dividends into the same security

**Key order fields:**
- Use `notional` (dollar amount) instead of `quantity` (share count) for dollar-cost averaging
- Supports `gtc` (good-till-cancelled) time-in-force for limit-based allocation strategies

## 4. Challenger bank with investing

**Scenario:** You run a challenger bank and want to offer integrated stock and ETF trading alongside your existing deposit accounts.

**What you'll build:**

1. Open a brokerage account tied to your existing user record ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts` with `corr_metadata`
2. Link your bank's account as the funding source ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/ach-relationships`
3. Power market data widgets ŌĆö quotes, logos, news ŌĆö `GET /v1/marketdata/real-time/{symbol}`, `GET /v1/marketdata/logos/{symbol}`, `GET /v1/marketdata/news/{symbol}`
4. Let users place orders from within your banking UI ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders`
5. Sync order and funding status back to your core system ŌĆö `POST /v1/webhooks`
6. Retrieve execution reports for internal reconciliation ŌĆö `GET /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders/{orderId}/executions`

**Key account field:**
- `corr_metadata` ŌĆö a freeform string (up to 255 chars) for storing your internal user or account ID alongside the Buildmarkets account. Makes cross-system lookups straightforward without a separate mapping table.

**Key market data endpoints:**
- Real-time prices, company profiles, stock logos, and news feeds are all available at the partner level ŌĆö no account context required
- Ideal for powering discovery and watchlist UIs

## 5. Employee investment benefits

**Scenario:** You're an HR or benefits platform and want to offer employees a brokerage account as a workplace benefit, funded via payroll integration.

**What you'll build:**

1. Open accounts with employer and employee metadata ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts` with `corr_metadata`, `registered_rep_code`
2. Set up ACH relationships linked to employee bank accounts ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/ach-relationships`
3. Initiate recurring payroll-linked deposits ŌĆö `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/funding/deposits`
4. Offer a curated investment menu using sector and security reference data ŌĆö `GET /v1/marketdata/reference/sectors` + `POST /v1/marketdata/reference/sector-symbols`
5. Deliver tax documents and statements to employees by email ŌĆö `POST /v1/documents/email`
6. Retrieve year-end documents including W-9s ŌĆö `POST /v1/documents/w9`, `POST /v1/documents/list`

**Key market data endpoints:**
- Use `POST /v1/marketdata/reference/equity-security-master` and `POST /v1/marketdata/reference/sector-symbols` to build a curated fund menu scoped to specific industries or sectors rather than exposing the entire universe of tradable securities

## Common integration patterns

### Webhooks over polling

"For operational state changes ŌĆö account KYC approval, order fills, deposit settlements ŌĆö use webhooks rather than polling." Configure a webhook endpoint via `POST /v1/webhooks` and subscribe to the specific event types you need. Polling is acceptable for UI-driven reads like balances and positions. See Webhooks Overview for the full event catalog and delivery guarantees.

### Idempotency with `client_order_id`

Pass a `client_order_id` on every `POST /v1/accounts/{accountId}/orders` request. If a network timeout forces a retry, Buildmarkets returns the existing order rather than creating a duplicate. A reliable scheme: `{your-user-id}-{symbol}-{side}-{date}-{sequence}`.

### Handling pending states

| Resource | Pending state | Active state |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Account | `SUBMITTED` ŌåÆ `APPROVAL_PENDING` | `ACTIVE` |
| ACH relationship | `PENDING` | `APPROVED` |
| Deposit / withdrawal | `PENDING` | `SETTLED` |

Use webhooks (or poll sparingly) to detect these transitions before presenting confirmations in your UI.

### `corr_metadata` for cross-system linking

"The `corr_metadata` field on an account is your bridge between Buildmarkets and your internal data model." Store your internal user ID, account reference, or any structured string there at account creation. This eliminates the need for a separate ID-mapping database and makes cross-system lookups straightforward.
