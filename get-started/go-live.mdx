Before you switch your integration to production, work through this checklist. Going live with Buildmarkets means real customer accounts, real money, and real regulatory obligations — it's worth taking the time to get each step right. This page covers everything you need to do before, during, and immediately after flipping to production.

<Warning>
  Never use sandbox API keys in production, and never point production traffic at the sandbox URL (`https://dev-tapp-api.tappengine.com`). The two environments are completely separate. Using sandbox credentials in production will result in authentication failures, and sending real customer data to sandbox is a compliance risk.
</Warning>

## Production base URL

```
https://api.tappengine.com
```

This is the only change required in your code to switch environments — replace the sandbox base URL with this one and swap in your production credentials.

## Pre-launch checklist

<Steps>

### Sign and return your partner agreement

Before Buildmarkets activates your production environment, you must have a signed partner agreement on file. This agreement covers your obligations as a partner using Buildmarkets's broker-dealer and clearing infrastructure, including customer suitability, supervision, and recordkeeping requirements.

If you haven't received your partner agreement or need to review its status, contact your Buildmarkets account manager or email [partners@buildmarkets.com](mailto:partners@buildmarkets.com).

### Generate production API credentials

Production API keys are separate from sandbox keys. Log in to the Buildmarkets Partner Dashboard, navigate to **Settings → API Keys**, and generate a new key pair explicitly labeled for production (e.g., `production-backend`).

Assign only the scopes your production services require. Follow the principle of least privilege — a backend service that only places orders and reads positions does not need `documents:write` or `keys:manage`.

| Scope | Grant if your app needs to… |
|---|---|
| `accounts:read` | Read account details, balances, and positions |
| `accounts:write` | Open and close investor accounts |
| `funding:read` | Read ACH relationships and deposit/withdrawal history |
| `funding:write` | Create funding sources and initiate deposits/withdrawals |
| `trading:read` | Read orders and order history |
| `trading:write` | Place, modify, and cancel orders |
| `marketdata:read` | Access real-time quotes and historical data |
| `documents:read` | List and retrieve account documents and tax forms |
| `documents:write` | Upload and email documents |
| `webhooks:read` | List webhook configurations |
| `webhooks:write` | Create and manage webhook endpoints |

Store your production secret immediately in a secrets manager (AWS Secrets Manager, HashiCorp Vault, or equivalent). The secret is shown only once and cannot be retrieved again.

<Warning>
  Never hard-code production API keys in source code, mobile app bundles, or client-side JavaScript. Always load them from a secrets manager or environment variables at runtime, server-side only.
</Warning>

### Configure production webhook endpoints

In sandbox, your webhook endpoint likely pointed to a local tunnel or a test URL. Before going live, register a stable, publicly reachable HTTPS endpoint and verify it can handle Buildmarkets event payloads at production volume.

```bash
curl -X POST https://api.tappengine.com/v1/webhooks \
  -H "X-API-Key: YOUR_PRODUCTION_KEY" \
  -H "X-API-Secret: YOUR_PRODUCTION_SECRET" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "name": "Production webhook",
    "url": "https://your-app.example.com/webhooks/buildmarkets",
    "eventTypeNames": [
      "kyc.verification.approved",
      "kyc.verification.rejected",
      "order.filled",
      "order.canceled",
      "transfer.deposit.completed",
      "transfer.deposit.failed",
      "transfer.withdrawal.completed",
      "transfer.withdrawal.failed"
    ]
  }'
```

Confirm your webhook handler returns a `200` status within the delivery timeout. Failed deliveries are retried, but persistent failures will result in event loss.

### Verify KYC flows with real identity data

In sandbox, KYC was controlled by SSN suffixes. In production, identity verification runs through a real identity verification provider. Test your compliance submission flow with real (non-test) identity data before soft-launching to customers.

Verify that your application:

- Surfaces a clear in-progress state while KYC is processing (accounts start in `onboarding` or `approval_pending`)
- Handles `kyc.verification.approved` and `kyc.verification.rejected` webhook events gracefully
- Gives users clear next steps if their account is rejected or requires manual review
- Does not allow funding or trading until the account reaches `active` status

### Confirm ACH and funding flows

Real ACH transfers take 1–3 business days to settle in production — not the instant settlement you saw in sandbox. Make sure your application:

- Shows users a realistic funding timeline (not "instant")
- Listens for `transfer.deposit.completed` rather than assuming settled funds after submission
- Prevents order placement until `buying_power` reflects the deposited amount
- Handles `transfer.deposit.failed` with a clear error message and retry path

### Perform a production smoke test

After completing the above steps, run a limited production smoke test before opening to all customers:

1. Create one investor account with real identity data
2. Confirm KYC approval via webhook
3. Link a real bank account via Plaid
4. Initiate a small deposit (e.g., $10) and wait for `transfer.deposit.completed`
5. Place a small market order once buying power is available
6. Confirm the fill via webhook and check the position

If all six steps complete successfully, your integration is ready for broader launch.

### Review your compliance obligations

As a Buildmarkets partner, you have regulatory obligations under the terms of your partner agreement. These typically include:

- **Customer suitability** — Collecting and retaining investment profile data for each account and ensuring orders are suitable for the customer's profile
- **Supervision** — Maintaining a supervisory system appropriate for the trading and advisory activity on your platform
- **Recordkeeping** — Retaining records of customer orders, account activity, and communications as required by FINRA and applicable SEC rules
- **Customer disclosures** — Delivering required regulatory disclosures (e.g., Form ADV, margin disclosures) before account opening and recording acceptance via `POST /v1/investors/{investor_id}/disclosures/acceptances`

Buildmarkets's underlying infrastructure is operated by a FINRA-registered broker-dealer and SEC-registered RIA. Your partner agreement specifies the division of compliance responsibilities between Buildmarkets and your firm. If you have questions about your specific obligations, contact [compliance@buildmarkets.com](mailto:compliance@buildmarkets.com).

</Steps>

## Environment comparison

| Attribute | Sandbox | Production |
|---|---|---|
| **Base URL** | `https://dev-tapp-api.tappengine.com` | `https://api.tappengine.com` |
| **KYC** | Simulated via SSN suffix | Real identity verification |
| **Orders** | Simulated fills | Live order routing |
| **ACH funding** | Instant, no real money | Real transfers, 1–3 business days |
| **API keys** | Sandbox-only credentials | Production-only credentials |
| **Customer data** | Use test data only | Real customer PII |

## Switching to production

Once you've completed the checklist, switching is a two-line change:

```bash
# Before (sandbox)
export BM_BASE="https://dev-tapp-api.tappengine.com"
export BM_KEY="your-sandbox-key"
export BM_SECRET="your-sandbox-secret"

# After (production)
export BM_BASE="https://api.tappengine.com"
export BM_KEY="your-production-key"
export BM_SECRET="your-production-secret"
```

No endpoint paths, request bodies, or response-handling code needs to change.
